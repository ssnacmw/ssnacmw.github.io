What is ACM?

The Association for Computing Machinery is a U.S.-based international learned society for computing. It was founded in 1947 and is the world's largest and most prestigious scientific and educational computing society. ACM is widely recognized as the premier membership organization for computing professionals, delivering resources that advance computing as a science and a profession; enable professional development; and promote policies and research that benefit society.

About Us

The SSN ACM Student Chapter is an official student body incepted in 2014 under the aegis of Department of Computer Science and Engineering, SSN. We are a group of dedicated CS students who are working towards promoting the knowledge of Computer Science to CS as well as non-CS students with the aim of improving general as well as specialized scientific interests. The chapter aims at uniting the computing fraternity at SSN under one tag and allowing the students to learn together and share their knowledge to cater the interests of the individuals as well as the institute as a whole. The chapter organizes a number of events including programming contests, talks by renowned speakers, workshops etc.which give the students an exposure to the competitive computing world as well as allow them to understand the advancements going on in the computing sphere worldwide.

Why join ACM Student Chapter?

  • Complimentary Subscription to Communications

  • A full-year electronic subscription to Crossroads, ACM’s Student Magazine
  • ACM Student Quick Takes (SQT) a quarterly email newsletter with each issue highlighting ACM activities, programs, and offerings of interest.
